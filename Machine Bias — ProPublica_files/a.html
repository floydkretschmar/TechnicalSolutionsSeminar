Handing a simplistic analysis like this off to non analysts without a very strong sense of it's error and overall limitations is just a set up for failure.

                                                                An obvious problem in general is that any system like this can only work on the data that is collected and the data that is collected comes from arrests and convictions, but if minorities are more likely to come to the attention of law enforcement, i.e. more likely to be arrested than whites per incident of law breaking, and more likely to be convicted than whites, then the models are going to pick up on that and of course be biased against minorities.

                                                                This is an obvious case of building a model on a biased sample, and thus getting biased results, which are self-reinforcing.

                                                                The problem with models like this is that the outcomes are not truly independent of the model. Models like this are used in insurance, and that's a lot less problematic, because in insurance it is the INSURED to files the claim that the models predict.

                                                                But with this type of model, it is the POLICE who take the action that is being predicted. So the police see the prediction, and then the police have a level of control over the outcome (at both an individual and sample level), whereas insurance companies don't have control over the outcome.

                                                                This is bad, bad, practice.

                                                                The problem here is that there is no good way to get truly unbiased data.

                                                                Hypothetically, let's use the following assumptions (totally made up numbers on my part):

                                                                Only 1 out of 20 incidents of breaking the law committed by whites results in an interaction with law enforcement.

                                                                1 our of 10 incidents of breaking the law committed by blacks results in an interaction with law enforcement.

                                                                When there is an interaction with law enforcement, for whites 1 out of 5 results in an arrest. For blacks 1 out of 3 results in an arrest.

                                                                When there is an arrest, for whites 1 out of 4 results in a conviction. For blacks 1 out of 2 results in a conviction.

                                                                I don't know what the real numbers are, but I suspect that the patter I've laid out above is somewhat close to the real world.

                                                                What a computer model REALLY needs to predict is the chance that a person will "break the law" in the future.

                                                                But of course, if most of the time that whites break the law they are never caught and prosecuted, then the computer model has no real knowledge of how often they break the law. The computer can only know about arrests and convictions. But if the system is biased against blacks in terms of arrest and conviction rates per law breaking incident, then of course the computer models will be too.

                                                                I think anyone would have to be a total fool not to acknowledge that middle-class and wealthy whites break the laws at far higher rates than poor people and minorities, per conviction.

                                                                In other words, for middle-class and wealthy whites, they probably break the law 50 to 100 times per conviction. In other words, out of every 100 times that they break the law, an actual conviction happens. But for poor blacks its probably more like 1 out of every 15 or 20 times. So blacks are being convicted at a MUCH higher rate per actual violation than whites, and of course this will heavily biases any computer model against blacks and just perpetuate the problem, as this article states.
